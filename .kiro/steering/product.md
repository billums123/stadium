# Product steering

## What STADIUM is

A mobile-first web app that turns any walk, run, or bike ride into a live
AI sports broadcast. The athlete hits GO, puts their phone in a pocket,
and an AI commentator narrates their pace, surroundings, and anything
they shout into the mic — over a generative crowd bed and (optionally) a
cinematic music bed.

## Who it's for

Primary: an athlete filming a 30-second vertical video of themselves in
the middle of a normal workout, intending to post it to X / TikTok /
Instagram / LinkedIn. They need the HUD and the commentary to be visibly
and audibly dramatic enough that a stranger's thumb stops mid-scroll.

Secondary: a viewer who encounters the clip on a feed. They need the
scoreboard to read in under 2 seconds without sound.

## Voice and tone

- **Commentary lines:** ESPN-meets-David-Attenborough, slightly British,
  absurdly hyperbolic. Never sincere for more than one sentence at a
  time. References "the crowd", "the scouts", "the bench", "the dog".
  Avoids corporate-tone AI filler.
- **UI copy:** short, chyron-like, all caps for labels, title-case for
  commentary. No emoji. No hashtags in the app itself — hashtags belong
  in the social post the athlete composes afterwards.

## Out of scope

- Multi-athlete / head-to-head broadcasts.
- Saved session history, user accounts, or any server-side persistence.
- Non-English commentary.
- Recommendations ("try running this route next"), coaching advice, or
  health metrics beyond distance / time / pace.
