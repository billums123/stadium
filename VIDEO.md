# The clip

The single most important submission artifact. Everything else is downstream
of whether this clip is good.

## The thesis

**Ordinary walk + broadcast-grade commentary = comedy.** The gag only lands
if the contrast is real — you must look like a person doing a boring
activity, and the app must sound like it was paid a lot of money to narrate
it.

Don't over-act. The dog not reacting is funnier than the dog reacting.

## Format

- **Length:** 28–35 seconds. Any longer and the algorithm drops off.
  Any shorter and the cold-open doesn't breathe.
- **Aspect:** 9:16 vertical. Film with the phone locked portrait on a
  chest strap, a friend's phone, or a mini-tripod on a bike handlebar.
- **Hook rule:** something must be visually happening in the first
  1.5 seconds or the scroll wins.

## Cast

- **You.** Dressed normally. Not workout cosplay. Regular clothes, regular
  shoes, regular face. You are about to have an extremely normal day.
- **A phone** in your pocket or chest strap, running STADIUM at full
  volume through its speaker (so the mic captures the commentary
  alongside ambient).
- **A second phone (or a friend with a phone)** filming you.

## Shot list — 30 seconds

| # | Time | Shot | Action | Audio |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0.0–2.0 s | **Hero frame, locked off.** You standing at a crosswalk or parked bike, phone in hand, deadpan. | You look down at phone, tap GO. | Silence → crowd bed fades in. |
| 2 | 2.0–6.0 s | **Medium, matched to your first steps.** | You start walking / jogging, same deadpan. | *"Good evening — welcome to STADIUM. Tonight's main event: TANNER."* |
| 3 | 6.0–10.0 s | **Close on phone screen.** HUD visible: athlete name, ON AIR, scoreboard. | Phone held out briefly, then pocket it. | Color voice: *"That's a debut — no prior broadcasts on record. Conditions: frankly ideal."* |
| 4 | 10.0–14.0 s | **Wide, you walking past mundane thing** (parking meter, pigeon, bus stop). | Normal walking speed, no acting. | Play-by-play: *"We are live in three. Two. One. And we are underway."* |
| 5 | 14.0–20.0 s | **Medium, you picking up to a light jog.** | Subtle pace change. | Pace-surge line fires: *"A shift. TANNER at 9.8. Somebody has decided today is the day."* |
| 6 | 20.0–24.0 s | **Environment cut** — a dog looking at you, a driver at a stop sign, a squirrel. | Cut to the environmental subject, then back to you. | Color aside: *"A squirrel just witnessed pace. It will tell its friends. Its friends will not believe it."* |
| 7 | 24.0–28.0 s | **Hold on your deadpan face**, still jogging. | No expression change. | Brief crowd roar; maybe one more hype line. |
| 8 | 28.0–30.0 s | **Phone reveal, big HUD visible.** | Hold the phone to camera, scoreboard in frame, then — *cut to black*. | — |

## Practical tips

- **Volume.** Play STADIUM through the phone speaker, not earbuds — your
  external-camera mic has to capture it. Test this at home first.
- **Permissions.** Start the broadcast at least once before filming so
  iOS has accepted the motion / mic permission; the prompts will ruin
  your take otherwise.
- **Chest strap or gorillapod.** If you don't own one, hold the phone
  very still at waist height with one hand — the HUD needs to be
  legible for shot 3.
- **Environment.** Urban + everyday is the brief. A park is fine. A
  track is *wrong* — looks like a workout, kills the contrast.
- **One take is fine.** This is not a Pixar short. The gag is that
  nothing special is happening.
