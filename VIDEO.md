# Filming the STADIUM demo clip

The video is the single most important submission artifact. Everything
else is downstream of whether it's a good 30 seconds.

---

## 0 · Which device should you use?

**Short answer: iPhone if you have one. Either works.**

| | iPhone (11 or later) | Modern Android (Pixel 6+, Galaxy S22+) |
|---|---|---|
| Built-in screen recording | Control Center → ⏺ (records system audio too) | Pull-down shade → "Screen recorder" |
| Camera mic quality | Noticeably cleaner, less wind noise | Varies by model — usually thinner |
| Stabilisation | Strong out-of-the-box | Usually fine |
| Lav mic accessory support | Universal (3.5 mm adapter or USB-C MagSafe) | USB-C only |
| STADIUM audio compatibility | Tested, works | Tested, works |
| AirDrop → Mac editing | Yes | Phone-to-computer via cable or Drive |

The clip doesn't need a great camera — it needs a **good visible HUD**
and **audible commentary**. Both phones handle that. iPhone just has
fewer footguns.

If you have both, put the app on the iPhone (for cleanest audio out the
speaker) and film with whatever you prefer. If you only have one phone,
see Option 1 below.

---

## 1 · Pre-flight on the app

Before you leave the house:

1. Open the deployed URL on the phone that will run STADIUM.
2. Grant motion + location permissions via the `GRANT ACCESS` primer
   card. If this is the first visit you'll see it; otherwise skip.
3. Tap **GO** once to verify: welcome plays → countdown fires → horn
   → live HUD appears. Tap **STOP** immediately to cancel. You don't
   want the first tap on-camera to be the first time this device has
   ever run the app — permissions prompts will ruin your take.
4. **Tap the top-right cog** and flip units to whatever you want in
   the camera (mph/km-h, yards/metres for the dash labels). Set your
   athlete name.
5. Pick a goal. For filming, `100 yd · 20 s` or `40 m · 8 s` works
   great because the entire session fits inside a single ~30s clip.
6. Turn phone to **silent / DND** (so no notification sound lands in
   your audio take).
7. Set the device volume to ~80 % — loud enough for the camera mic to
   catch from 1–2 ft away, not so loud it distorts.
8. Charge to >50 %. The session itself is cheap but warming up a
   cold phone in the cold can drain fast.

## 2 · Setup options

Pick the one that matches your gear.

### Option 1 — Solo, one phone (simplest)

Gear: your phone. Nothing else.

The trick: **use screen recording to capture the HUD, not the camera**.

1. Start system **screen recording** *before* the session. On iPhone:
   Control Center → long-press ⏺ → "Microphone On" → Start. On Android:
   pull-down shade → Screen recorder → enable mic.
2. Prop the phone on something stable at chest height — a wallet, a
   backpack, a bench, a mini tripod. It needs to see the HUD clearly
   for about 8–10 seconds while the cold-open + horn plays.
3. Walk into frame, tap **GO** on camera. Stand still and listen to
   the welcome + countdown + horn. The screen recording captures HUD
   + system audio together.
4. Once the horn fires, pick up the phone and keep walking/running
   with it visible (held in front of you at arm's length, or clipped
   into a chest strap).
5. Let it run 20–30 s. Hit **STOP**. Screen record captures the
   FINAL WHISTLE recap screen — that's your closing shot.

This gives you a single vertical clip with app audio baked in. No
second camera, no editing gymnastics. The only compromise is there's
no shot of you *as a person* running — it's a point-of-view clip.

### Option 2 — Solo, two phones (better, more dramatic)

Gear: two phones. One runs STADIUM (call it **APP**), one films you
(call it **CAM**).

1. Put **APP** in a chest strap, front pocket, or waist band. Volume
   up, screen awake (the app holds a wake lock anyway).
2. Set **CAM** on a mini tripod, selfie stick, or prop it on
   something stable. Frame yourself at roughly waist-to-head so viewers
   can see you *and* catch the app audio from the speaker.
3. Start **CAM** recording.
4. Walk into frame, pull **APP** out, tap **GO** so the camera can
   see the HUD. Stow APP back in chest strap / pocket once the horn
   fires. Alternative: leave APP visible in your hand the whole time
   — that's the "I'm filming myself using a ridiculous app" POV.
5. Walk / run the session. The app audio plays through APP's speaker
   and CAM picks it up. If wind is loud, turn your chest or palm
   toward CAM to shield the speaker.
6. Hit **STOP** on APP (via its on-screen button) on-camera so CAM
   sees the recap transition. Hold the pose for ~3 s so there's
   footage of the FINAL WHISTLE screen, then walk CAM back and stop
   recording.

The two-phone setup is dramatically better because viewers see *you
as a human doing this absurd thing*, which is the comedy. It also
lets you do reaction shots — deadpan face into camera after a hype
line lands is the key viral beat.

### Option 3 — With a friend (cleanest)

Same as Option 2 but your friend holds CAM. They can track you as you
move, get different angles, and stop naturally when you stop.

One rule: **they film in portrait (9:16), not landscape**. Tell them.

## 3 · Audio capture strategies, ranked

### 3a · Phone speaker → camera mic (default, works)

Play the STADIUM audio out of APP's speaker. CAM's built-in mic picks
it up from 1–3 ft away. Compression + room tone + wind are the cost;
"good enough for a 30 s comedy clip" is the benefit. This is what
most demos use.

Tips:
- Keep APP within 2 ft of CAM's mic when a big line hits (cold-open
  welcome, horn, pace surge, recap).
- Turn your body slightly so APP's speaker points at CAM. Phone
  speakers are directional.
- Avoid windy spots. Even light wind destroys this setup.

### 3b · Wireless lav mic on you (best sounding)

Gear: DJI Mic, Rode Wireless Go, or equivalent. ~$150 new, often
under $100 used.

1. Clip the transmitter to your collar. Clip the receiver into
   CAM's USB-C or 3.5 mm jack.
2. Play STADIUM audio out loud through APP's speaker as usual — the
   lav catches it from 6 inches away, not 2 ft.
3. You get clean, full-range audio with almost no wind.

This is the single biggest upgrade available for the clip. If you're
borrowing a friend's gear for anything, borrow this.

### 3c · Voiceover pass (backup / cleanup)

If your field audio comes out unusable — wind, traffic, mic static —
you can:

1. Screen-record a clean STADIUM session at home (Option 1 setup) to
   get a pristine audio track.
2. Film the running footage separately with your external camera.
3. Edit the clean audio onto the visual in iMovie / CapCut.

This is a safety net, not a primary plan. Field audio with a bit of
city ambience reads as *authentic*; a voiceover over silent running
footage reads as *produced*. You want authentic.

## 4 · Shot list — 30 seconds

A suggested structure you can film in one take with the Option 2
setup:

| # | Time | Shot | Action | Audio moment |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0.0 – 2.5 s | Hero frame, locked off | You standing, phone in hand, deadpan into CAM | Silence → crowd bed fades in |
| 2 | 2.5 – 6.0 s | Close on phone screen | Tap GO, HUD visible | Welcome line: *"Good evening, welcome to STADIUM…"* |
| 3 | 6.0 – 9.5 s | Medium shot, you motionless | The countdown runs, 3 · 2 · 1 | Beep · beep · beep |
| 4 | 9.5 – 10.5 s | Cut to wide | You take off walking/running | **HORN**, session clock starts |
| 5 | 10.5 – 17 s | Walking beat, environment shot | Whatever's around you — a dog, a crosswalk, a pigeon | Commentary reacts to pace |
| 6 | 17 – 22 s | Pace change | Pick up to a light jog | Pace-surge line fires, music volume swells |
| 7 | 22 – 27 s | Hold on your deadpan face | Still jogging, no expression change | Color commentator drops a dry aside |
| 8 | 27 – 30 s | Phone reveal + tap STOP | Bring phone close to camera, tap STOP | FINAL WHISTLE chrome visible, closing line |

---

## 5 · Composition rules

- **Vertical 9:16.** Full stop. Every major platform rewards it.
- **First 1.5 s has to catch the eye.** Start on your face, locked off.
  A scroller on X has to see *a person* within half a beat.
- **No acting.** The comedy is the contrast between a normal human on
  a normal walk and professional-sports-grade narration. If you ham
  it up, the joke dies.
- **No music pre-added.** STADIUM already generates driving music for
  you. Don't double up.
- **Don't pan the phone too fast.** Phone speakers are directional —
  swinging APP around dumps audio behind you.

## 6 · Export + edit (if needed)

For a clean single-take clip (Option 1 or a two-phone sync): minimal
editing. Trim the head and tail, maybe boost audio in CapCut or iMovie,
export at 1080×1920 MP4. Upload to YouTube / TikTok / IG Reels / X
natively.

For a two-phone setup where you want to intercut: align the two
tracks to the air-horn blast (it's distinct and loud — easy sync
marker). Then cut freely.

Keep the final under 35 s. Instagram Reels and TikTok both cap
engagement on anything longer at this point.

## 8 · Minimum-viable checklist before you press record

- [ ] Deployed URL open on APP, permissions granted, test-tapped GO
      once
- [ ] APP volume ~80 %, DND on
- [ ] Goal picked (`100 yd · 20 s` or `40 m · 8 s` recommended)
- [ ] Units match what you want on-screen
- [ ] Athlete name set
- [ ] CAM in 9:16 portrait, recording, mic armed
- [ ] Something stable for CAM if you're solo
- [ ] Lav mic charged and paired, if using one (3b)
- [ ] No wind / not in direct sun on the phone screen
- [ ] You've practised the take once dry

Good luck. If the first take is bad, the second is almost always
better. If the third is bad, move to a quieter spot.
